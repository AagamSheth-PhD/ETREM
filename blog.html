Tips & Tricks

5 Rules to Make Your Perfume Last All Day

You spray your fragrance in the morning and by noon it's gone. Sound familiar? The trick isn't spraying more — it's spraying smarter.

  • Moisturise first. Fragrance clings to hydrated skin. Apply an unscented lotion before your perfume.
  • Target pulse points. Wrists, neck, behind the ears, inner elbows — these areas radiate heat and amplify the scent.
  • Don't rub. Rubbing breaks down the top notes and shortens longevity. Let it dry naturally.
  • Layer with matching products. If you have a body wash or lotion in a similar scent family, use it as a base.
  • Spray on clothes too. Fabric holds fragrance longer than skin — especially effective on scarves and collars.

Fragrance Guide

Understanding Fragrance Families: Which One Are You?

Every perfume belongs to a fragrance family — a broad category that defines its character and mood. Knowing yours is the fastest way to find your perfect scent.

  • Woody: Earthy, warm, grounding. Think cedarwood, vetiver, patchouli. Best for confident, classic personalities. Try: The Warden, The Forge.
  • Oriental/Spicy: Rich, sensual, exotic. Built on resins, amber, vanilla, and spices. For those who make an impression. Try: The Poem, The Affair.
  • Aquatic/Fresh: Clean, breezy, effortless. Sea notes, citrus, and ozonic elements. Perfect for day wear and summer. Try: The Expanse, The Reign.
  • Gourmand: Delicious and sweet — think coffee, chocolate, and almond. Modern and playful. Try: The Murk, The Genesis.
  • Floral: Timeless and romantic. Rose, jasmine, and muguet. A universal crowd-pleaser. Try: The Muse.

Styling

Day vs Night: How to Choose Your Fragrance

Your scent should shift with the sun. Here's a simple guide to matching your fragrance to the moment:

  • Morning & Office: Go light and fresh. Aquatic, citrus, and green notes won't overwhelm a closed room. Try: The Expanse, The Muse.
  • Evening & Date Night: Turn up the intensity. Oriental, gourmand, and deep woody fragrances come alive in the warmth of the evening. Try: The Poem, The Affair, The Murk.
  • Summer: Fresh and aquatic scents feel natural when it's hot — avoid heavy musks that can become cloying. Try: The Reign, The Expanse.
  • Winter: Warm, rich scents like vanilla, amber, and spice feel like a cosy wrap. Try: The Poem, The Forge, The Murk.
